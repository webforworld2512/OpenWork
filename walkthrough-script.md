# OpenWork — Spoken Walkthrough Script
**Duration:** ~2.5 minutes | **Tone:** Conversational, professional, demo-style

---

## 1. INTRO (30 seconds)

Here's something most organizations get wrong about feedback.

We spend millions on engagement surveys, pulse checks, performance reviews — and what do we end up with? A number. A 3.7 out of 5. Maybe a traffic-light dashboard. But none of that actually tells a manager *what* to do differently on Monday morning.

Meanwhile, the people closest to the problem — the employees — have real, specific, actionable observations. They know what's working. They know what isn't. But there's no safe way to surface that — and even if there were, no one's turning it into a development plan.

That's what OpenWork does. It takes anonymous, structured feedback from employees and uses AI to turn it into personalized coaching and learning plans for managers. No scores. No ratings. Just growth.

Let me show you how it works — starting with the manager's experience.

---

## 2. MANAGER VIEW — Main Focus (~60 seconds)

When a manager logs in, this is what they see. *(show /my-growth)*

Notice what's *not* here. There are no numbers. No star ratings. No charts or trends. That's by design. We don't want managers counting responses or reverse-engineering who said what. This is a growth space — not a scorecard.

When they open **My Development Insights** — *(click into /my-growth/insights, generate)* — the AI produces a full leadership profile based entirely on aggregated anonymous feedback.

It starts with a **Development Summary** — a narrative overview of how their leadership is experienced by their team.

Then it breaks down their **Key Strengths** — what the team values most. Things like "creates a psychologically safe environment" or "effective at mentoring senior people into leadership roles."

Below that, **Growth Opportunities** — not criticisms, but areas where focused attention could make a real difference. Framed as coaching, not evaluation.

There's also a **Behavioral Insights** section — patterns the AI has identified. For example: "Communication style is most effective in structured settings; less consistent in ad-hoc conversations." That's the kind of nuance a survey score could never give you.

And finally, **Recommended Next Steps** — concrete actions they can take immediately.

Now, if they want a longer-term roadmap, they go to **My Learning Plan**. *(show /my-growth/learning)*

This gives them a **30-60-90 day improvement plan** — structured, specific, time-bound. Plus **curated learning resources**: books, workshops, courses — all tailored to the growth areas the AI identified.

Every page includes a privacy disclaimer. No individual responses are ever shown. No scores. No counts. The manager cannot trace anything back to anyone.

---

## 3. TRANSITION (10 seconds)

So — where does all of this insight come from?

---

## 4. EMPLOYEE VIEW (30 seconds)

It comes from the employees. *(show /employee/feedback)*

Employees log in, select their business unit and manager, and submit structured feedback — rating five leadership dimensions and adding written comments about what's working well, what should improve, and any suggestions.

The key word here is **anonymous**. Their identity is never stored with the feedback. Never. And insights only appear once enough responses have been collected — so no one can be singled out. Employees are told this upfront, which is what makes the feedback honest.

---

## 5. LEADERSHIP VIEW (30 seconds)

Leadership sees something different. *(show /admin)*

They get the organizational view — aggregated ratings, trend lines over time, distributions across business units. They can filter by department or time range, trigger AI summaries for any manager, and manage the organizational structure.

But even here — they never see raw, individual feedback. Only aggregates. The system enforces anonymity at every level.

---

## 6. CLOSING (15 seconds)

So that's OpenWork. We take something every organization already has — employee observations about their managers — and we turn it into something most organizations don't have: **personalized, AI-powered leadership development plans** that are private, safe, and actually actionable.

We're not measuring managers. We're growing them.

---

*End of script.*
